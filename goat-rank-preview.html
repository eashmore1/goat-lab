Free vs GOAT Pass

Everyone earns XP and levels up — that's the retention hook that keeps people playing daily.

The GOAT Pass badge becomes the rank chip: the plain 🐐 goes away, and instead pass holders show their gold-bordered rank chip on the public leaderboard. The gold border signals "pass holder," the color & title signal how good they are — one badge doing both jobs, and it levels up over time.

Free players see their own rank & XP bar on their home screen — they always know how close the next rank is. They just don't get the chip on the public leaderboard, so they watch everyone else flexing theirs. That's what makes them want the Pass.

Everyone gets a level-up moment — hit a new rank and you get a celebration either way (that's the hook that makes free players want to show it off). Pass holders get the upgraded animated version.

+ Pass holders earn 25% more XP, so they climb the ranks noticeably faster — another reason the Pass feels worth it. (The exact % is easy to tune.)

At launch: nobody starts from zero

Existing players start at the rank they've already earned. On launch we do a one-time backfill — every account's XP is computed from its history (dailies played, streaks kept, scores posted, trophies unlocked) so loyal players open the update already sitting at, say, All-Star instead of Rookie.

Without this, someone with 60 dailies behind them opens the app as a "Rookie" and feels punished for playing early. The backfill makes the rollout feel like a reward, not a reset.
